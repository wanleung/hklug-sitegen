SUSE Rolls Out Agama 21 Installer with Enhanced UEFI Support and Deployment Safeguards

By Staff Reporter · 2024-05-21 18:48:00

SUSE has released version 21 of its Agama installation framework, incorporating new enhancements aimed at modernizing the operating system installer used across its enterprise and community distributions. According to reporting by Phoronix, the update introduces support for systemd-boot and adds validation warnings for specific installation configurations.

Agama serves as the foundational installer for both SUSE Linux Enterprise (SLE) and openSUSE Linux distributions. The latest iteration, version 21, continues engineering efforts to refine the tool used for deploying these operating systems.

A key addition in Agama 21 is support for systemd-boot. This provides an alternative to traditional bootloader options, aligning with modern UEFI standards. The update also addresses deployment safety by implementing warnings for no-desktop installations. Administrators proceeding with desktop-free setups will now encounter prompts designed to prevent accidental misconfiguration during the provisioning process.

These enhancements are part of ongoing work by SUSE engineers to improve the installer's reliability and feature set. The Agama framework is designed to bridge physical hardware and operational readiness for the latest SUSE Linux Enterprise and openSUSE Linux distributions.

As the update becomes available, it marks the latest step in the development of the modern Agama installer. Users are advised to consult official documentation for specific details regarding the new bootloader support and validation prompts.
