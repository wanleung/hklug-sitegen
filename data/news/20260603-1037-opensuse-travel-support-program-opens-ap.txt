Title: openSUSE Travel Support Program Opens Applications for Asia Summit 2026
Content:
<p>The openSUSE project has opened applications for its Travel Support Program (TSP) ahead of the openSUSE.Asia Summit 2026, offering financial assistance to speakers who need help covering travel costs to attend the regional conference.</p>
<p>The TSP, funded through donations to the Geeko Foundation, provides travel grants specifically designated for presenters at openSUSE community events. According to an announcement published on the openSUSE news site on 3 June 2026, the application window is now open, though a specific closing date was not detailed in the initial notice.</p>
<p>The program is a recurring initiative within the openSUSE ecosystem designed to ensure that geographic and financial barriers do not prevent community contributors from sharing their work at major gatherings. The Geeko Foundation, which manages the program's funding, allocates resources based on available donations and the volume of applications received.</p>
<p>The openSUSE.Asia Summit is one of the project's flagship regional events, bringing together developers, system administrators, and open-source enthusiasts from across the Asia-Pacific region. These summits typically feature technical talks, workshops, and collaborative sessions covering a range of open-source topics.</p>
<p>For IT professionals and open-source contributors in the region — including those in Hong Kong — the TSP represents an opportunity to present technical work or research without the burden of self-funded international travel. The program underscores the openSUSE community's commitment to lowering participation barriers and fostering a globally inclusive contributor base.</p>
<p>Speakers interested in applying are encouraged to visit the official openSUSE TSP portal for eligibility criteria and application details. The project has historically urged potential attendees to apply early, as funding is finite and allocated on a rolling basis.</p>
<p>The announcement serves as a reminder that community-driven projects like openSUSE rely not only on code contributions but also on financial donations that directly enable broader participation. Contributors who wish to support future TSP rounds can do so through the Geeko Foundation.</p>
<hr>
<p>openSUSE 專案已開放其旅行資助計劃（TSP）的申請，為即將舉行的 openSUSE.Asia Summit 2026 提前準備，旨在為有需要幫助承擔參加地區會議旅費的講者提供財務支援。</p>
<p>這項名為 TSP 的計劃，其資金來自對 Geeko 基金會的捐款，專門為 openSUSE 社群活動的演講者提供旅費補助。根據 openSUSE 新聞網站於2026年6月3日發佈的公告，申請窗口現已開放，惟初步通知中並未具體說明截止日期。</p>
<p>該計劃是 openSUSE 生態系統中一項常設的倡議，旨在確保地理和財務障礙不會阻礙社群貢獻者在主要聚會中分享他們的工作。負責管理計劃資金的 Geeko 基金會，會根據可用捐款和收到的申請數量來分配資源。</p>
<p>openSUSE.Asia Summit 是該專案的旗艦地區活動之一，匯聚來自亞太區的開發者、系統管理員及開源愛好者。這些高峰會通常包含技術演講、工作坊及協作會議，涵蓋一系列開源相關主題。</p>
<p>對於該地區的資訊科技專業人士及開源貢獻者——包括香港的從業者——TSP 提供了一個展示技術工作或研究成果的機會，而無需自行負擔國際旅行的費用。該計劃彰顯了 openSUSE 社群致力於降低參與門檻、培育全球包容性貢獻者群體的承諾。</p>
<p>有興趣申請的講者，應瀏覽官方的 openSUSE TSP portal 以獲取資格標準及申請詳情。該專案歷來都鼓勵潛在與會者盡早申請，因為資金有限，且以滾動方式分配。</p>
<p>這項公告提醒我們，像 openSUSE 這樣的社群驅動專案，不僅依賴代碼貢獻，也需要財務捐款，才能直接促成更廣泛的參與。希望支持未來 TSP 週期的貢獻者，可通過 Geeko 基金會進行捐助。</p>
