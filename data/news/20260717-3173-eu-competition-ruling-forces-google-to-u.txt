Title: EU Competition Ruling Forces Google to Unlock Android's Core for Rival AI Assistants
Content:
<p>The European Commission has delivered a landmark order, compelling Google to grant rival AI assistants the same deep access to Android's core features that its own Gemini enjoys. The ruling targets approximately 11 specific capabilities slated for Android 18, striking at the heart of Google's structural advantage by mandating the opening of the camera, microphone, screen content, and background app controls to competitors.</p>
<p>The decision, reported by The Hacker News, goes further by requiring the activation of a "wake-word" feature, allowing an AI to respond even when the screen is off. These are the fundamental building blocks of a seamless, always-available AI assistant. Google must implement this mandated interoperability in the next major release, Android 18, with full compliance required by August 1, 2027. In a related DMA obligation, Google must also share anonymized Search data with competitors, further loosening its grip on the Android ecosystem.</p>
<p>The implications are global in scale. With Android commanding roughly 70% of the world's smartphone market, the technical framework established for EU compliance will inevitably become the de facto standard. Global OEMs like Samsung and Xiaomi, which build their interfaces atop Android, will logically adopt these changes worldwide for consistency. Thus, the competitive spark ignited in Europe is set to illuminate markets across Asia and beyond.</p>
<p>For the developer ecosystem, this is a watershed moment. By breaking down Google's walled garden, the ruling aims to catalyze a new wave of innovation. Third-party AI assistants can now pursue powerful, automated workflows that were previously technically infeasible—such as using the camera to identify an object and then seamlessly executing related tasks across other applications without user intervention.</p>
<p>The long-term impact will hinge on the precise security and permissions architecture Google designs to provide this mandated access. Critical questions remain about how the company will balance this forced openness with app security and user privacy. The manner in which Google navigates compliance versus legal challenge will fundamentally shape the future of AI competition on the world's most popular mobile operating system.</p>

<p>歐洲委員會發出具里程碑意義的命令，強制谷歌允許競爭對手的AI助理，獲得與其自身Gemini同等深度的Android核心功能訪問權限。這項裁決針對約11項計劃納入Android 18的具體功能，直指谷歌結構性優勢的核心，要求其向競爭者開放相機、麥克風、螢幕內容及背景應用程式的控制權。</p>
<p>據The Hacker News報道，該決定更進一步要求啟用「喚醒詞」功能，使AI即使在螢幕關閉時也能作出回應。這些功能構成了一個無縫、始終可用的AI助理的基本組件。谷歌必須在下一個主要版本Android 18中實施此強制互操作性，並須在2027年8月1日前完全合規。作為DMA相關義務的一部分，谷歌還必須與競爭對手分享匿名化的搜尋數據，進一步放鬆其對Android生態系統的控制。</p>
<p>其影響是全球性的。由於Android佔據全球智能手機市場約7成份額，為符合歐盟要求而建立的技術框架，勢必會成為既成事實的標準。全球OEMs如三星和小米，均在Android基礎上構建自身介面，從全球一致性考量，合乎邏輯地會在全球範圍採用這些更改。因此，在歐洲點燃的競爭火花，將照亮亞洲及其他地區的市場。</p>
<p>對開發者生態系統而言，這是分水嶺時刻。通過打破谷歌的walled garden，這項裁決旨在催化新一輪創新浪潮。第三方AI助理現可追求強大且自動化的工作流程，而這些在技術上曾是不可行的——例如使用相機識別物體，然後無需用戶干預，便能在其他應用程式中無縫執行相關任務。</p>
<p>長期影響將取決於谷歌設計用以提供此強制訪問權限的精確安全與權限架構。關於公司如何平衡此被迫的開放性與應用程式安全及用戶隱私，仍有關鍵問題懸而未決。谷歌在合規與法律挑戰之間的應對方式，將根本性地塑造這款全球最流行移動操作系統上AI競爭的未來。</p>
